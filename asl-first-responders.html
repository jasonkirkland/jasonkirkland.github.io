🤟 ASL for First Responders

American Sign Language phrases for police & fire personnel communicating with the Deaf community

How to use this guide: Each card shows a key emergency phrase in American Sign Language (ASL). Follow the numbered steps to form the sign, then practice the motion shown by the ↑ blue arrows. ASL uses facial expressions too — raise your eyebrows for questions!
1

HELP

Call for / offer assistance

How to sign it

  • 1Hold your non-dominant hand flat in front of you, palm facing up.
  • 2Make a fist with your dominant hand and point your thumb straight up (like a thumbs-up).
  • 3Place your fist on top of your flat palm.
  • 4Lift both hands upward together in one smooth motion.
💡 Tip: This sign looks like one hand is "helping" the other rise up — a great visual reminder!
2

STOP

Halt / cease movement

How to sign it

  • 1Hold your non-dominant hand flat in front of you, palm facing up.
  • 2Hold your dominant hand flat and vertical (like a karate-chop hand), fingers together.
  • 3Chop down sharply with your dominant hand onto the palm of your non-dominant hand.
💡 Tip: Think of the edge of your hand as a stop bar coming down. A firm, quick chop makes the sign clearer and more urgent.
3

ARE YOU HURT?

Pain / injury assessment

How to sign it

  • 1Point both index fingers at each other (all other fingers curled in). Leave a small gap between the fingertips.
  • 2Twist both index fingers inward toward each other simultaneously — a quick back-and-forth rotation.
  • 3To ask "Where does it hurt?", first sign HURT, then point to areas of the body questioningly (raise eyebrows).
💡 Tip: The twisting motion represents a sharp, stabbing pain sensation. Raise your eyebrows to turn it into a yes/no question.
4

YOU ARE SAFE

Reassurance / all clear

How to sign it

  • 1Make two fists (S-handshape) with both hands.
  • 2Cross your wrists in front of your chest, like a big X, with one arm on top of the other.
  • 3Sweep both hands outward at the same time, rotating them so your palms end up facing forward (away from you).
  • 4For "YOU are safe," first point at the person before signing SAFE.
💡 Tip: The motion looks like you're opening a pair of curtains. Calm facial expression reinforces the reassuring message.
5

COME WITH ME

Direct someone to follow you

How to sign it

  • 1Extend your index finger upward (all other fingers curled in) with your dominant hand.
  • 2Curl your index finger inward toward your body — a natural beckoning gesture. Repeat once or twice.
  • 3To say "COME WITH ME," first point to the person, then make the beckoning sign, then point to yourself.
  • 4You can also nod in the direction you want them to go while signing.
💡 Tip: The beckoning finger motion is nearly universal — most people will understand it immediately, even without ASL knowledge.
